Cal Wilson

Welcome to Cal Wilson's professional website! He is a graduate student who plans to specialize in stress and anxiety in the workplace to improve worker efficiency and job satisfaction among employees in various industries.

About MeEmploymentResearch ExperienceCurrent

Recently receiving his B.S. in Psychology and minor in Sociology from West Virginia University, Cal is interested in studying phenomena occuring in everyday social interactions such as in the home, school system and workplace. He earned the President's Award in Spring 2016 and made the deans list in [2016*]. He has previously presented a poster at the 2018 Eastern Psychological Association Annual Conference Poster Session on negative work-to-family spillover predicting 10-year mortality risk in middle-aged adults, and is currently assisting researchers at West Virginia University study emotion regulation in parents and their adolescents in rural Appalachia. Cal's career goal is to be an internal bussiness consultant that applies modern research on industry, company, and individual factors in private organizations to improve worker efficiency and job satisfaction. He is enrolled in the Masters of Science in Industrial Relations (MSIR) program at WVU and will continue to assist with research during his time there.
